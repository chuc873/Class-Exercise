About The Project

This platform is a vessel for research, reflection, and growth surrounding Marshall Islands' monumental efforts to combat the climate crisis for the world. Structured much as an atlas, here you are invited explore a series of visualizations of Marshall Island's history, progression, and activism concerning the global climate crisis.

In 2015, Marshall Islands made its name in fighting for a more progressive Paris Agreement. Since then, RMI has declared a national climate crisis and continues to play an outsized role in the global fight for keeping global warming under 1.5°C. Whilst the various campaigns focus on the level of challenge for islanders to overcome this crisis, it is important to recognize the role optimism towards Pacific culture and resilience plays in this fight. A unique history of fighting for nuclear justice and an abundance of global-local activism puts Marshall Islands at the forefront.

What would it look like to map hopes and dreams of a better future amidst our current climate? Can mapping optimism and resilience from Marshallese communities reshape how the world thinks about—and act about—the climate crisis?

Who created this platform?

My name is Ching-Yu Daniel Chu, a Taiwanese graduate student currently at Parsons School of Design in New York. I created this project as part of my MS degree thesis. I landed in this cause initially when researching the formation of Marshallese communities in the United States, focusing on history, causations, and trends in Northwest Arkansas.

I would like to create a story-based map which illustrates riMajel relationships with climate and environment. The hope is that the answers from the community can help reframe global perceptions of climate crisis not only on scientific facts but also its human impacts, and put Marshallese communities at the forefront of both global challenges and solutions. This form functions as a space for gathering responses form the Marshallese community to highlight your intimacy with nature past, present, and future.

The following pages will contain questions about you, your beliefs, and positions. The public facing part of the project will ask you to submit a media file of any kind with accompanying descriptions and geography. You are not obligated to answer all questions, but only those that you feel comfortable with. I would encourage you to take some time with your answers if possible. The stories you share will be illustrated in an interactive map. Please be mindful that information you shared could be shared publicly unless otherwise noted or requested. The map will be published on a web platform along with an atlas depicting Marshallese history in relation with society and environment, and where the current fights against this crisis stand, all of which are part of my thesis project.

Disclaimer

I am not Marshallese and have yet to visit the Marshall Islands. The research works conducted throughout the project is through a combination of literature/media reviews, attendance at online presentations/conferences, talking with some community members, etc, during various levels of lockdown and quarantine in New York City. As a fellow citizen of the Pacific, I stand in solidarity with all independence and decolonial movements from Okinawa to Aotearoa to Hawai'i. I also recognize that the API community is not a monolith as seen and presented where I reside. I hope this project can work to highlight and elevate the importance of Pacific Islanders within the US context. Although this work tackles more climate-side of issues, I want to make the larger political intentions of Pacific solidarity and sovereignty clear. When it is finally safe to travel post-pandemic, I hope to return to our ocean and be one of the first on a flight to Majro!

In addition, I also acknowledge the incredible pain and suffering Covid-19 has brought to the Pacific Islander communities. There are arguably more urgent concerns to deal with at the moment, especially for many Marshallese families that live in the US. There is no pressure to participate in this survey. What and how you answer the prompts are all in your control and subject to your changes at any time. You have always hold all rights with the information you share. I am immensely honored to be able to share and hopefully amplify Marshallese voices in any way.

You may reach out for any additional questions, concerns, and comments at danielchu@newschool.edu.
